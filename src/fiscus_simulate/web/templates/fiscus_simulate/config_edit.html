Balance matrix (year 1, rows by account). Each account's holding = the asset-class total × its proportion (fractions 0–1); the taxable account is the remainder. Expected income = Σ (balance × annual income yield) for that account (gross investment income). Total income adds active pensions — only on the Total row (pensions aren't attributed to an account). After-tax income nets each row by its tax treatment: taxable less dividend/interest tax; tax-deferred × (1 − withdrawal rate) as a spendable proxy (it is only taxed when withdrawn); tax-free untaxed; the Total row also nets pensions by other_pension × taxable_fraction. Est. tax on income now is the taxable account's dividend/interest tax at t=0.

Pension income / yr sums every person's income streams at their real amounts; active now counts only streams whose owner has already reached start_age; starts in N yr is the wait to the earliest not-yet-started stream.

Withdrawal rate. Gross = planned spending ÷ household wealth (the classic anchor). Net now = (planned spending − income available today) ÷ wealth, where income today is pensions active now plus gross portfolio income — the real early drawdown before later pensions start. Approximate: it uses gross portfolio income and ignores that tax-deferred income isn't freely spendable until sold.

At retirement (all real, no inflation/PV). Household retires in N = the latest person's retirement_age − current_age; until then each person saves annual_real_savings/yr into the pool. Est. assets = W₀(1+r)N + Σ each person's savings grown as an annuity at the portfolio real return r = Σ (asset weight × real return); contributions compound over that person's own working years then sit to retirement. Retirement income = total real pensions + est. assets × portfolio income yield. Spending vs. retirement assets = planned spending ÷ est. assets (the at-retirement withdrawal rate); coverage = retirement income ÷ spending.
